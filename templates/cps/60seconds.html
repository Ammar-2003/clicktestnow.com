Understanding the 60-Second CPS Test

The 60-second CPS test measures your clicking speed and endurance over a full minute. This extended duration test evaluates not just your raw speed but also your ability to maintain consistent performance over time.

The 60-second duration is ideal for comprehensive clicking assessment because:

  • Endurance Measurement: Tests your ability to maintain speed over an extended period
  • Realistic Scenario: Matches the duration of many gaming situations requiring sustained clicking
  • Comprehensive Analysis: Provides data on performance trends (starting speed, consistency, fatigue)
  • Skill Development: Helps identify areas for improvement in both speed and stamina

Clicking Techniques

Different clicking techniques can be used during the 60-second test. Here are the most common approaches:

  • Standard Clicking: Using one finger to click at a consistent pace
  • Jitter Clicking: Vibrating your arm or hand muscles for extremely high short-term CPS
  • Butterfly Clicking: Using two fingers alternately on the same mouse button
  • Drag Clicking: Dragging fingers across the mouse button to register multiple clicks

Each technique has advantages and disadvantages for a 60-second test. The best approach depends on your physical endurance and clicking goals.

Performance Benchmarks

Here are typical performance levels for the 60-second CPS test:

  • Beginner: 4-6 CPS (240-360 total clicks)
  • Intermediate: 6-8 CPS (360-480 total clicks)
  • Advanced: 8-10 CPS (480-600 total clicks)
  • Expert: 10-12 CPS (600-720 total clicks)
  • Professional: 12+ CPS (720+ total clicks)

Remember that consistency is just as important as peak speed in a 60-second test. A steady 8 CPS throughout will yield better results than starting at 12 CPS but dropping to 4 CPS due to fatigue.
