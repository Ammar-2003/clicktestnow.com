Understanding CPS Test

CPS (Clicks Per Second) is a measure of how fast you can click your mouse in one second. This 8-second test is designed to measure your sustained clicking speed over a longer period.

The 8-second duration is ideal for CPS testing because:

  • Accuracy: Longer duration provides a more accurate average CPS
  • Stamina Measurement: Tests your ability to maintain speed over time
  • Technique Evaluation: Allows different clicking techniques to be properly assessed
  • Fair Comparison: Standardized duration for fair global comparisons

Clicking Techniques

Different clicking techniques can significantly impact your CPS score. Here are the most common techniques:

  • Regular Clicking: Using one finger to click (typically 5-7 CPS)
  • Butterfly Clicking: Using two fingers alternating on the mouse button (8-12 CPS)
  • Jitter Clicking: Vibrating your arm muscles for rapid clicks (10-15 CPS)
  • Drag Clicking: Dragging fingers across the mouse button (15-25 CPS)

Each technique requires practice to master. Experiment to find which works best for you!
