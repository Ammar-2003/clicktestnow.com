Understanding 20-Second CPS Test

The 20-second CPS (Clicks Per Second) test measures how many times you can click your mouse in a 20-second period. This extended duration test evaluates both your clicking speed and endurance.

The 20-second duration is ideal for measuring clicking performance because:

  • Endurance Assessment: Tests your ability to maintain speed over a longer period
  • Realistic Scenario: Matches the duration of many gaming situations requiring sustained clicking
  • Technique Evaluation: Allows time to demonstrate different clicking techniques
  • Comprehensive Measurement: Provides a more accurate representation of your overall clicking ability

Clicking Techniques

There are several techniques you can use to maximize your CPS score:

  • Regular Clicking: Standard clicking with one finger
  • Butterfly Clicking: Using two fingers alternately on the same mouse button
  • Jitter Clicking: Vibrating your arm or hand muscles to achieve high speeds
  • Drag Clicking: Dragging your finger across the mouse button to register multiple clicks

Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. Remember that consistency and endurance are key factors in the 20-second test.
