What is Reaction Time

Reaction time is the interval between the presentation of a stimulus and the initiation of a response. This test measures your simple reaction time - how quickly you can respond to a visual stimulus.

Average reaction times vary by age and other factors:

  • Excellent: 150-200ms (top 1%)
  • Good: 200-250ms (above average)
  • Average: 250-300ms (typical adult)
  • Below Average: 300-350ms (room for improvement)
  • Slow: 350ms+ (may need practice)

How to Improve Reaction Time

Reaction time can be improved with practice and proper techniques:

  • Focus: Maintain concentration on the test area
  • Anticipation: Learn to anticipate the stimulus without clicking too early
  • Relaxation: Stay relaxed but ready to respond
  • Practice: Regular practice can improve neural pathways
  • Sleep: Proper rest significantly improves reaction times

Professional gamers and athletes often have reaction times in the 150-200ms range through extensive training and practice.
